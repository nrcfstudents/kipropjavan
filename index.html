description of the games

Football
The main objective in football is for one team to score more goals than the other within a set period of time (usually 90 minutes, divided into two 45-minute halves). The team with the most goals at the end of the game wins.
Rugby
Rugby is a contact team sport that originated in England and has become popular in many countries around the world. It is known for its physicality, teamwork, and distinctive style of play. There are two main types of rugby: rugby union and rugby league, with some differences in rules and gameplay, but the basic premise remains similar.
Basketball
Basketball is a fast-paced team sport in which two teams of five players each compete to score points by shooting a ball through a hoop. It is one of the most popular sports globally, played both professionally and recreationally. The game emphasizes skill, agility, teamwork, and strategy.
Hockey
Hockey is a fast-paced team sport played on ice or field, depending on the type of hockey. There are two primary forms of the game: ice hockey and field hockey, each with different rules, equipment, and playing surfaces. Both versions are popular around the world, with ice hockey particularly popular in countries with colder climates and field hockey more widespread in parts of Europe, Asia, and Oceania.
Volleyball
Volleyball is a fast-paced team sport where two teams of six players each compete to score points by sending a ball over a net and into the opponent’s court. The game is characterized by teamwork, strategy, and quick reflexes. It’s played both indoors and outdoors (beach volleyball) and has become one of the most popular sports worldwide.
